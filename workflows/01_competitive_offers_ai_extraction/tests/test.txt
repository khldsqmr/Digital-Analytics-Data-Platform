COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE HEADLINES May 29, 2026
Postpaid & Cable MVNO Key Highlights
1. AT&T
• Build-A-Plan to Launch 5/27: AT&T’s Build-A-Plan launched on May 27 but as of publication there are no links to the offer on AT&T’s website; an interested
party must know the URL for the service to sign up. One customer who attempted to sign up when the service became available seemed to be in a doom loop
and unable to receive support to get the service up and running. (5/27 - )
• Pullback on GS26 Series Offers: AT&T removed its $1300 w/$230 trade discount for the Galaxy S26 series leaving the max offer at $1100 ($900 for the S26
base model) with $95 min. trade – and reduced the $35 trade level to $500 (was $900); the $500 offer is still the max offer for AT&T Value 2.0 plans. AT&T also
removed the Any, Galaxy, Any Condition construct from its Galaxy trade-in offer.
2. Verizon
• iPhone 17 Offers Change – Verizon brought back its no trade-in required offers on Friday, May 29, but with some minor changes from prior offers. The top
offer, $1,000 off, is limited to the Unlimited Ultimate plan, Unlimited Plus grants an $830 discount and Unlimited Welcome (as of Friday) has no offer. There is
a new trade-in offer for Unlimited Welcome which grants customers up to $830 off with trade, but a port-in is required.
• Watch + Tablet On Us Bundles Return – Verizon brought back its phone + watch + tablet “on us” bundle for “Dads & Grads” as of Thursday, May 28. There is no
end date listed for these offers like there was for the Mother’s Day bundles.
3. Spectrum Mobile
• No changes
4. Xfinity Mobile

• No changes

• Up to $50 off Phones: 25% off any full-priced phone (max discount $50) w/activation on any plan and code NASCARFAN at checkout (5/22 – 5/28); not combinable w/other offers
4. Total Wireless
• iPhone 16e $79 In-Store: Total Wireless has reduced its iPhone 16e to $79 in stores (was $99) with a required port and 2-months of the $65 Total All Access plan (mid-May – TBD).
